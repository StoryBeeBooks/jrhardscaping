Last Updated: November 27, 2025

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are downloaded to your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They allow the Website to recognize your device and store some information about your preferences or past actions.

  • First-Party Cookies: These are set directly by us to make the website function.
  • Third-Party Cookies: These are set by other domains (e.g., Google, Microsoft, or YouTube) that we use for specific services like maps, analytics, or video playback.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to improve the functionality of our interactive tools (like the Solution Finder), analyze traffic to improve our search rankings, and protect the security of our forms. We categorize our cookies as follows:

A. Strictly Necessary Cookies (Essential)

These cookies are necessary for the Website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you, such as:

  • Setting your privacy preferences.
  • Filling in the "Contact" or "Consultation" forms.
  • Ensuring security by detecting malicious bot activity.

B. Functional Cookies

These allow the Website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization.

  • The "Problem Picker" Tool: If you use our Solution Finder tool (e.g., selecting "Sinking Driveway"), a cookie may remember your selection so you do not lose your place if you navigate to a product page and back.
  • Video Playback: We use high-resolution video headers. Cookies help buffer this content efficiently based on your connection speed.

C. Performance & Analytics Cookies

These allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site.

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to understand which "City Pages" (e.g., Vaughan vs. Toronto) are most popular and which "Solutions" users search for most. This data is aggregated and anonymized.
  • Microsoft Bing Webmaster Tools: We use Microsoft technologies, including Universal Event Tracking (UET) tags. This allows us to understand how users find our website via the Microsoft Search Network (Bing, Yahoo, AOL) and helps us troubleshoot how our site is crawled and indexed.

D. Marketing & Targeting Cookies

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They do not store directly personal information but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device.

  • Purpose: They may be used to show you relevant ads for JR Hardscaping or our retail division LuxeStone on other sites based on the products you viewed here.

3. Third-Party Technologies

We use trusted third-party services that may set their own cookies:

  • Google Maps: Used on our "Contact" and "Service Area" pages to display our office location and service radius.
  • YouTube / Vimeo: Used to serve the high-quality project walkthrough videos in our Hero sections and Portfolio.

4. How to Manage or Block Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer.

Specific Opt-Out Links:

In addition to browser settings, you can manage your preferences with our specific analytics partners:

  • Google: You can opt-out of Google Analytics across all websites by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Accessibility Policy

Last Updated: November 27, 2025

JR Stone Elite Ltd. ("Company", "we", "us", or "our"), operating as JR Hardscaping, is committed to ensuring equal access and participation for people with disabilities. We are committed to treating people with disabilities in a way that allows them to maintain their dignity and independence.

We believe in integration, and we are committed to meeting the needs of people with disabilities in a timely manner. We will do so by removing and preventing barriers to accessibility and by meeting our accessibility requirements under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) and Ontario's Integrated Accessibility Standards Regulations.

1. Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)

We are actively working to ensure our website, www.jrhardscaping.ca, conforms to the World Wide Web Consortium's (W3C) Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA.

These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with a wide array of disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, language, learning, and neurological disabilities.

2. Accessibility Features on Our Website

To ensure our site is usable by everyone, we have implemented the following features:

  • Keyboard Navigation: Our website, including the "Solutions" dropdown menu and Contact forms, can be navigated using a keyboard for users who cannot use a mouse.
  • Alternative Text (Alt Text): As a visual-heavy site featuring stone textures and project galleries, we strive to include descriptive Alt Text attributes on all meaningful images. This allows users using screen readers (such as JAWS, NVDA, or VoiceOver) to understand the content of our photos.
  • Contrast Ratios: We have designed our site using high-contrast typography (Dark Grey/Black text on White backgrounds) to ensure text is readable for users with low vision or color blindness.
  • Form Labels: All input fields on our "Contact" and "Quote" forms utilize descriptive labels to assist assistive technology.
  • Resizable Text: Our website is built using relative sizing, allowing users to zoom in up to 200% without loss of content or functionality.

3. Third-Party Content

Our website makes use of third-party services to provide certain functionality. We cannot always control the accessibility of content provided by these third parties, though we strive to choose partners who share our commitment to inclusion.

  • Google Maps: Used for our "Service Area" and location display.
  • YouTube/Vimeo: Used for project showcase videos.

4. Feedback and Accommodation Requests

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of the JR Hardscaping website. If you encounter any barriers while using our site, or if you require any content (such as a PDF brochure or warranty document) in an alternative, accessible format, please contact us.

We are committed to providing accessible formats and communication supports in a timely manner and at no additional cost.

Contact us to report an issue or request an accommodation.

5. Ongoing Monitoring

We regularly review our website and digital practices to ensure ongoing compliance with AODA standards. This policy is reviewed annually to ensure it reflects our current practices and legislative requirements.
